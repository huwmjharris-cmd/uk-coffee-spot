About Edinburgh Coffee Spot

A guide to Edinburgh’s coffee scene, built by a coffee-obsessed student.

Edinburgh Coffee Spot started because I kept finding myself wandering into coffee shops with no idea what was behind the counter. As a student, I spent a lot of time hopping between cafes looking for good coffee, and I quickly realised that what makes or breaks a flat white isn’t just the beans — it’s the machine pulling the shot.

I started keeping notes. Which shops had La Marzoccos, which ones were running Slayers, where to find a Victoria Arduino. I’d ask baristas, check Instagram stories, peer over counters. And I realised I wasn’t the only one who cared about this stuff. Plenty of people — whether they’re specialty coffee regulars or just curious — want to know what equipment a shop is working with before they walk in.

So I built this. Edinburgh Coffee Spot maps out as many coffee shops as I can find across Edinburgh and the surrounding areas, and where possible, documents the espresso machines they’re using. It’s not perfect — machines change, shops open and close, and some places keep their setup under wraps. But it’s a start.

If you’re a coffee shop owner and want to let people know what you’re running, or if you spot something that’s wrong, get in touch via the contact page. This is a community thing.
